Rule-Based Scenario Classification Using Vehicle Trajectories

Sungmo Ku, Jinho Lee

ISPRS International Journal of Geo-Information, 15(1), 37 — https://doi.org/10.3390/ijgi15010037

OpenSCENARIO Trajectory Classification Rule-Based ISO 26262 ISO 21448 (SOTIF) CARLA

Ensuring the safety of autonomous driving systems (ADS) requires scenario-based testing that reflects the complexity and variability of real-world driving conditions. However, the nondeterministic nature of actual traffic environments makes physical testing costly and limited in scope, particularly for rare and safety-critical scenarios. To address this, simulation has become a core component in validation by providing scalable, controllable, and repeatable testing environments.

This study proposes a trajectory-based scenario classification framework that emphasizes both generality and interpretability. Specifically, we define a set of rule-based maneuver classification criteria using lateral acceleration patterns and apply them to simulated urban driving scenarios modeled with OpenSCENARIO. To address overlapping maneuver characteristics, a priority ordering of classification rules is introduced to resolve ambiguities.

The proposed method was evaluated on a dataset comprising 7 types of maneuvers, including straight driving, lane changes, turns, roundabouts, and U-turns. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of rule-driven classification based on vehicle trajectory dynamics and highlight the potential of this approach for structured scenario definition and validation in ADS simulation environments.

Key Contributions

  • Trajectory-centric scenario classification framework — A rule-based maneuver classification method using lateral acceleration patterns to distinguish 7 driving maneuver types in urban environments, ensuring physical interpretability unlike black-box learning models.
  • Simulation dataset based on OpenSCENARIO v1.2 — A comprehensive dataset covering 7 trajectory-based urban driving scenarios designed for repeatability, coverage analysis, and future ADS testing.
  • Rule prioritization mechanism — A priority ordering strategy to resolve ambiguities arising from overlapping maneuver characteristics during classification.

Methodology of Disturbance-Based Scenario Classification in Simulation Environments

Sungmo Ku, Jinho Lee

Tech University of Korea, Department of Smart Factory Engineering & Department of Computer Engineering

OpenSCENARIO Disturbance Classification SAE J3016 Simulation PEGASUS

Autonomous driving systems have advanced toward higher levels of automation, particularly SAE J3016 Level 4 and above, where simulation-based training is increasingly emphasized. Through simulation, diverse driving scenarios can be reproduced, and consequently, various classification frameworks have been proposed. Previous ontology-based approaches that utilize road types and surrounding traffic object information face limitations: difficulty in comprehensively capturing all contextual information and high computational costs.

This study proposes a methodology that establishes a concise and clear foundation for classifying driving scenarios. The objective is to define classification criteria that enable the effective reproduction and training of hazardous scenarios, thereby supporting the learning process of autonomous vehicles. Using ASAM OpenX (developed as part of the German PEGASUS Project), disturbances implementable in simulation are classified into three stages: perception, decision-making, and control.

Key Contributions

  • Disturbance taxonomy across three ADS stages — Classifies simulation disturbances into Perception (occlusion, weather, illumination), Decision-Making (traffic participant interactions, speed limit changes, lane changes, pedestrian presence), and Control (slopes, sharp curves).
  • Identification of ADS vulnerability areas — Pinpoints which stages of autonomous driving are most susceptible to real-world disturbances, providing a practical foundation for improving robustness in simulation-based training.
  • OpenSCENARIO-based implementation — Leverages OpenSCENARIO to incorporate dynamic vehicle behavior into simulation data for extracting vehicle motion and performance metrics under disturbance conditions.
